Summary

In simple terms: LUST Chain is an EVM-compatible blockchain with native LST, LQC public mining, transparent genesis allocations, WLST/LUSDT liquidity, fixed staking reserves, locked creator vesting, NFT community infrastructure, and a progressive path toward treasury decentralization.

LUST Chain is an EVM-compatible blockchain designed to combine low fees, public contracts, fair participation, transparent liquidity formation, NFT community infrastructure, creator-economy tools, and a progressive path toward community-controlled treasury systems.

The network uses a native consensus and public participation model called LQC. LQC organizes public block participation through registration, activation, activity checks, heartbeat/active presence, producer selection, and fallback rules. Its core principle is simple: one registered and active wallet equals one valid participation chance. Stronger CPU or GPU power does not multiply a participant’s chance.

The native asset is LST. The initial genesis supply is 15,000,000 LST. Future supply increases only through protocol block rewards. The base reward begins at 2 LST per block, with the first scheduled reduction at block 6,000,000.

LUST Chain has already organized major public structures: staking vault, creator vesting, Genesis Liquidity Event, LUSDT, WLST, swap router/factory, P2P, NFT contracts, bridge contracts, community treasury, and dedicated reserve wallets.

The economic launch sequence is designed to be orderly: technical bootstrap, Genesis Liquidity Event, WLST/LUSDT liquidity creation, then public staking and public mining. This avoids unrestricted pre-liquidity circulation and aligns the network around transparent liquidity and public participation.

1. Introduction

1.1 Motivation

Many blockchain launches suffer from unclear allocations, weak initial liquidity, excessive control by private groups, hidden operational balances, unfair mining advantages, and poor separation between technical deployment and public economic launch.

LUST Chain follows a different path. The network separates technical bootstrap from economic activation and uses public contracts, public wallets, and public allocation labels to organize staking, liquidity, bridge operations, NFT communities, creator incentives, operational reserves, and future treasury governance.

1.2 Objective

The objective of LUST Chain is to provide blockchain infrastructure capable of supporting:

  • EVM-compatible smart contracts, ERC-20 tokens, ERC-721 NFTs, and ERC-1155 assets;
  • low-fee transactions using the native asset LST;
  • LQC public mining with one-wallet-one-chance participation;
  • staking funded by a fixed 5,000,000 LST genesis reserve, with no extra staking minting;
  • WLST/LUSDT liquidity and swap infrastructure;
  • LUSDT bridging from Polygon and BNB Smart Chain;
  • NFT memberships, Rabbit Club, Pioneer Rewards, creator tools, token factory, and P2P markets;
  • progressive movement from operational wallets into public contracts, multisigs, timelocks, and rule-based treasury systems.

4. Vision and Principles

4.1 Vision

LUST Chain was conceived as a long-term network for LST, LUSDT, NFTs, creator tools, public mining, staking, token creation, P2P markets, and community-driven growth. The goal is not simply to run a chain, but to build an auditable ecosystem where liquidity, rewards, reserves, vesting, and community funds are visible and progressively governed by transparent mechanisms.

4.2 Core principles

  • Fair participation: public mining should not be dominated by expensive hardware.
  • Transparent allocations: major genesis allocations should be publicly named and traceable.
  • Liquidity-first economic launch: public staking and public mining open only after initial liquidity.
  • No arbitrary issuance: new LST is created only by protocol block rewards after genesis.
  • Contract-based trust: staking, vesting, liquidity, bridge, NFTs, and treasury structures should move into public contracts when possible.
  • Community growth: Rabbit Club, Pioneer Rewards, public rewards, campaigns, miners, builders, and creators are part of the long-term ecosystem.
  • Progressive decentralization: operational wallets may exist early, but the long-term direction is contracts, multisigs, timelocks, and community governance.

5. General Network Architecture

LUST Chain combines the following layers:

  • Consensus and production: LQC registration, active participation, producer selection, committee rewards, fallback, and emergency bootstrap producers.
  • Execution: EVM smart contracts for LST, WLST, LUSDT, NFTs, staking, presale, swap, P2P, token factory, and creator applications.

6. LQC Consensus

6.1 Purpose of LQC

LQC is the native consensus and public participation model of LUST Chain. It is designed to organize block production, maintain network continuity, and distribute rewards without turning the chain into a permanent hardware war.

The core participation idea is one wallet = one mining identity. A registered and active wallet represents one valid public mining identity. Additional CPU or GPU power does not multiply the chance of that same wallet being selected.

6.2 Active participation

LQC rewards active participation, not passive registration. A wallet must be registered, activated, online, and recently active under the protocol heartbeat/activity rules to remain eligible.

  • Registered but inactive wallets do not receive mining rewards.
  • Offline or stale wallets are ignored automatically.
  • Official infrastructure wallets are not treated as public mining wallets.
  • The network includes automatic fallback protection so it can continue even if public miners go offline.

6.3 Economic launch rule

Public mining is tied to the economic launch sequence. The intended order is: Genesis Liquidity Event, liquidity creation, then public staking and public mining. This prevents unfair pre-liquidity access and keeps public rewards aligned with transparent liquidity and market formation.

6.4 Why LQC is different

LQC is not designed as a traditional hashrate contest and it is not a capital-weighted staking system. Its public mining layer is designed around active participation. A valid wallet must be registered, activated, synchronized, online and recently active to remain eligible.

  • Compared with traditional PoW: extra hardware power does not multiply the chance of the same wallet.
  • Compared with PoS: eligibility is not based on holding the largest amount of LST.
  • Compared with passive rewards: registration alone does not earn mining rewards.
  • Compared with fixed validator sets: the public layer is designed to admit many active community miners over time.

6.5 Identity and anti-abuse note

One wallet = one mining identity means one eligible protocol identity, not a guarantee that one wallet equals one unique human being. Because anyone can create multiple wallets, LUST Chain relies on active miner requirements, activation rules, heartbeat checks, maturity windows, bounded queues, monitoring and future anti-abuse improvements to reduce Sybil-style farming.

This makes the model more accessible than industrial mining, while remaining honest about the fact that no permissionless wallet-based system can perfectly prove one human per wallet without additional identity assumptions.

7. Mining Model, Payments and Rewards

LUST Chain uses a native mining and reward system designed to support public participation, network continuity, active participation, deterministic selection, and long-term emission control. The mining reward is paid directly by the protocol in native LST.

The system behaves differently depending on whether there is only one active public miner, multiple active public miners, thousands of active miners, or no active public miners at all.

7.1 Block time

LUST Chain targets an average block time of approximately 15 seconds per block. At this target, the network produces approximately 2,102,400 blocks per year. Actual block time may vary depending on network conditions, node connectivity, and synchronization.

7.2 Initial block reward

Before the first reward reduction, each valid block has a base reward of 2 LST per block. This reward is distributed according to the active mining state of the network.

7.3 Public mining reward split

When public mining is active and there is a valid public mining committee, the base block reward is split as follows:

  • 70% to the selected active block producer.
  • 30% to the selected active public miner committee.

With the initial reward of 2 LST per block, this means 1.40 LST goes to the selected active block producer and 0.60 LST goes to the selected active public miner committee.

The producer of the block does not receive the 30% committee share for the same block. The producer receives the producer share. The committee share is reserved for the eligible active public committee selected for that block.

7.4 The 30% pool is not paid to every registered wallet

The 30% committee reward is not paid to every wallet that registered once. It is not a passive reward and it is not automatically paid to every miner in the network at the same time.

The 30% committee reward is not paid to:

  • inactive wallets;
  • offline miners;
  • wallets with expired heartbeat;
  • wallets that stopped mining;
  • old mining wallets no longer active;
  • official infrastructure wallets;
  • prod-1 and prod-2 emergency wallets.

The 30% reward pool is paid only to eligible active public miners selected for that block’s reward committee.

7.5 How the 30% committee reward is divided

For each block, the protocol calculates the 30% committee reward and divides it equally among the selected committee members for that block.

Example with a 2 LST block reward:

  • Total block reward: 2.00 LST
  • Producer share: 1.40 LST
  • Committee reward pool: 0.60 LST

The same proportional rule applies after reward reductions.

7.6 Why not pay all thousands of miners in every block?

LUST Chain is designed to support thousands of miners, but paying every active miner in every single block would be inefficient and would make each individual reward extremely small.

Instead, LUST Chain uses a rotating active committee model:

  1. thousands of miners may be active in the network;
  2. each block selects an eligible producer;
  3. each block selects an eligible reward committee;
  4. only the selected committee for that block shares the 30% pool;
  5. the committee rotates over time through deterministic protocol rules.

This keeps the network scalable while still rewarding active public participation.

7.7 Deterministic committee selection

The reward committee is selected by the protocol using deterministic blockchain rules based on chain state, block data, and active miner eligibility. This makes the reward process transparent and verifiable.

  • No centralized server manually chooses who receives the 30%.
  • No administrator manually distributes mining rewards.
  • The reward is calculated and paid directly by the protocol during block finalization.

7.8 Active miner eligibility

A miner must be active to be eligible for the producer position or the committee reward. A miner is considered eligible only when all required conditions are met:

  1. the wallet is registered in the LUST mining registry;
  2. the wallet has completed activation;
  3. the miner is online;
  4. the miner has a recent valid heartbeat;
  5. the miner is inside the protocol activity window;
  6. the miner is not offline, exited, or stale;
  7. the miner is not an official emergency infrastructure wallet.

A registered wallet alone is not enough. The miner must remain active.

7.9 Offline miner rule and network freeze protection

If a miner goes offline, stops sending heartbeat, disconnects, or becomes stale, that miner is removed from active reward eligibility. Offline miners do not receive the 30% reward pool, do not block the network, and do not freeze block production.

This is important because LUST Chain may have thousands of miners, and many of them may disconnect at different times. The protocol is designed to continue operating safely even when many miners are offline.

To support thousands of miners safely, the active public queue is bounded. This prevents a large number of offline wallets from blocking the chain.

7.10 One wallet, one mining identity

LUST Chain public mining is based on active wallet participation, not raw hashrate dominance. Each registered and active wallet represents one mining identity.

Additional CPU or GPU power does not increase the mining chance of the same wallet. This makes mining more accessible and prevents the system from becoming a pure hardware competition.

7.11 Single active public miner scenario

If only one public miner is active and eligible, that miner may receive the full mining reward for the block because there is no separate active committee to share the 30% pool.

Example before the first reward reduction:

  • Base reward: 2 LST
  • Only one active public miner: the active public producer may receive the full 2 LST block reward, plus applicable transaction fees.

This avoids wasting rewards when there are not enough active public miners to form a separate committee.

7.12 Multiple active public miner scenario

If two or more public miners are active, the normal split applies:

  • 70% to the selected active producer.
  • 30% to the selected active public committee.

Example before the first reward reduction: with a 2 LST base reward, the producer receives 1.40 LST and the selected committee shares 0.60 LST.

7.13 Thousands of active miners scenario

If thousands of miners are active at the same time, they do not all receive a payment in every block. Instead, the protocol selects an eligible producer and a limited reward committee for each block.

Over time, committee selection rotates through active miners according to deterministic rules. This balances fairness, scalability, predictable reward distribution, network performance, and long-term decentralization.

7.14 Emergency fallback mode

If there are no active public miners available, the network enters emergency fallback mode. In emergency fallback mode, official infrastructure may produce blocks only to keep the network alive.

Official emergency wallets do not receive normal mining profit. When an official emergency block is produced, the full block reward is routed to the Community Treasury, not to the official producer.

This keeps the chain alive without allowing official infrastructure to accumulate passive mining rewards.

7.15 Official wallet reward rule

The official wallets are infrastructure wallets, not public miners. They may be used only as fallback or emergency producers when public miners are not available.

  • They do not participate in the public 30% active miner reward pool.
  • They do not receive passive rewards while public mining is active.
  • They do not receive emergency mining profit.
  • Emergency rewards are directed to the Community Treasury.

7.16 Community Treasury role

The Community Treasury receives rewards from emergency blocks when the network has no active public miners. This design prevents rewards from being lost to inactive wallets or accumulated by official infrastructure.

The Community Treasury may be used for ecosystem growth, public incentives, liquidity, infrastructure, and long-term network support according to the official treasury policy.

Community Treasury address: 0x30aF2866A6e1A193e17a95924C15aA19d13E759F

7.17 Transaction fees

Transaction fees are separate from the base mining reward. Transaction fees go to the block producer and are not part of the 30% committee reward pool.

The selected block producer receives:

  1. the producer share of the base block reward; and
  2. applicable transaction fees from the block.

The committee receives only its share of the protocol mining reward.

7.18 Reward reduction at block 6,000,000

LUST Chain includes a scheduled block reward reduction starting at block 6,000,000. The first reward reduction lowers the base block reward from 2 LST per block to 1 LST per block.

This reduction controls long-term inflation and makes LST emission more scarce over time. At a 15-second block target, block 6,000,000 is approximately 2.85 years after genesis.

After block 6,000,000, the base reward becomes 1 LST per block.

7.24 Reward examples at permanent tail emission

When the reward reaches the permanent minimum reward of 0.125 LST per block, the normal public mining split becomes 0.0875 LST to the producer and 0.0375 LST to the selected committee pool. If no public miner is active, the full 0.125 LST emergency reward goes to the Community Treasury.

7.25 Public mining release policy

Public mining should only be released after the protocol is stable and the LQC public mining safety layer is active and confirmed. This safety layer is the release gate for opening public mining safely to the community.

Before public release, the network must confirm:

  1. LQC public mining safety layer activation has passed successfully.
  2. Blocks continue normally after activation.
  3. RPC endpoints are synchronized.
  4. The explorer is indexing blocks correctly.
  5. Official emergency fallback works.
  6. Offline miners cannot freeze the chain.
  7. Active miners are the only wallets eligible for the 30% reward pool.
  8. Public miner packages are updated and verified.

Public staking and public mining remain locked for the community until the first WLST/LUSDT liquidity pool is created. The intended order is presale, then liquidity, then public staking and public mining.

16.2 Community Treasury

The Community Treasury may receive rewards generated by official producers during bootstrap, when no eligible public miners are active or before public mining is opened economically. These rewards are treated as community resources, not as a permanent private advantage.

Because protocol rewards can accumulate over time, the live Community Treasury balance may be higher than its 2,000,000 LST base allocation. The live balance should be checked on the explorer.

16.3 Future treasury contract

The Community Treasury may remain as a public operational wallet during the early phase. As the network matures, community-controlled funds are intended to migrate into public smart contracts, multisig structures, timelocks, or decentralized governance mechanisms.

The long-term goal is to reduce dependency on any single operator and progressively move community resources toward transparent, auditable, and rule-based on-chain management.
