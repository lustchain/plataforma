Overview

This Risk Disclosure explains the main technical, operational, economic, legal, and ecosystem risks associated with LUST Chain and its official services, including lustchain.org, explorer.lustchain.org, rpc.lustchain.org, rpc-backup.lustchain.org, and platform.lustchain.org.

LUST Chain is an experimental, community-driven blockchain project. Participation in the network, use of ecosystem tools, interaction with smart contracts, and acquisition, holding, staking, wrapping, bridging, or transferring of LST or wLST involve significant risk.

By using the Services, you acknowledge that you understand these risks and accept full responsibility for your decisions.

1. General Project Risk

LUST Chain is an emerging blockchain ecosystem with evolving infrastructure, tools, smart contracts, economic mechanisms, and public participation layers. As a result, users should expect uncertainty, rapid iteration, incomplete tooling, and potential design changes over time.

No aspect of the project should be interpreted as guaranteed, final, permanent, or free from defects.

2. Technical & Software Risk

  • Bugs may exist in nodes, wallets, smart contracts, explorers, RPC infrastructure, or ecosystem applications.
  • Software upgrades, parameter changes, or unexpected behavior may cause instability or service interruption.
  • Transactions may fail, behave unexpectedly, or interact with contracts in unintended ways.
  • Indexing, explorer data, RPC responses, or application-layer information may be delayed, incomplete, or inaccurate.

Even when reasonable efforts are made to test and review code, no software system can be assumed to be free of vulnerabilities or defects.

3. Smart Contract Risk

Interacting with smart contracts on LUST Chain involves significant risk. Contracts may contain coding errors, design flaws, integration failures, economic vulnerabilities, oracle issues, accounting mistakes, or permission misconfigurations.

  • Staking contracts may behave unexpectedly.
  • Claim contracts may fail or require emergency fixes.
  • Wrapping contracts such as wLST may be affected by implementation or accounting errors.
  • NFT, marketplace, creator economy, or payment contracts may have undiscovered issues.

Users should assume that any smart contract interaction can result in partial or total loss of funds.

4. Wallet & Key Management Risk

You are solely responsible for your wallets, private keys, seed phrases, browser extensions, devices, and signing behavior.

  • If your keys are lost, your assets may be permanently inaccessible.
  • If your wallet is compromised, your assets may be stolen.
  • If you sign malicious or incorrect transactions, approvals, or contract calls, funds may be lost irreversibly.

LUST Chain does not custody user keys and cannot recover lost credentials.

5. Network Participation & Production Risk

LUST Chain is designed around LQC as its protocol direction for organized public participation in network production. However, participation accessibility does not eliminate network-level risk.

  • Bootstrap operation may differ from public participation phase behavior.
  • Production, fallback handling, or reward distribution may evolve over time.
  • Users with ordinary hardware may still face uptime, connectivity, or performance limitations.
  • Network conditions may affect reward probability, participation quality, or producer behavior.

There is no guarantee that participation in network production will always be profitable, stable, or equally accessible under every network condition.

6. Market, Liquidity & Price Risk

  • LST and wLST may be highly volatile.
  • There may be limited or no liquidity at certain times.
  • Prices may move sharply in either direction or collapse completely.
  • Public markets may remain thin, fragmented, or illiquid even after launch.

The existence of public liquidity, wrapped assets, DeFi integrations, or ecosystem applications does not guarantee price support, market depth, or exit opportunities.

7. Presale & Claim Risk

If you participate in Genesis Community Sale or related ecosystem claim processes, you accept risks associated with contribution tracking, claim timing, refund flow, contract import logic, and launch execution.

  • Presale imports may require manual batch processing.
  • Claim availability depends on launch execution and contract readiness.
  • Bonus unlocks may be delayed, changed, or impacted by technical issues.
  • Refund processes may depend on chain-specific contract state and user action.

Participation in any presale or claim structure should never be treated as risk-free.

8. Staking & Yield Risk

Staking is not a guaranteed source of profit. It involves smart contract risk, token price risk, liquidity risk, unlock timing risk, and reward model risk.

  • Rewards may be lower than expected.
  • Locked assets may become illiquid during market stress.
  • Token price decline may outweigh nominal staking rewards.
  • Emergency contract behavior or governance intervention may affect normal flows.

Any expectation of yield must be treated as speculative and uncertain.

9. wLST & DeFi Integration Risk

wLST is intended as the wrapped ERC-20-compatible representation of native LST for DeFi and application-layer integrations. Wrapping and unwrapping mechanisms may involve contract, accounting, or liquidity risk.

  • Wrap / unwrap functions may fail or be paused.
  • Integrated DeFi protocols may introduce additional third-party risk.
  • Liquidity pools using wLST may suffer slippage, impermanent loss, or poor market depth.
  • Application-layer assumptions about wLST may be incorrect or unsafe.

10. Infrastructure & Service Availability Risk

Official services such as the website, explorer, public RPC, secondary RPC, WebSocket endpoints, and ecosystem hub may become unavailable, degraded, rate-limited, or inaccurate.

  • RPC endpoints may fail, be congested, or respond inconsistently.
  • Explorer data may lag or display incomplete information.
  • Apps and websites may be interrupted by hosting, networking, or deployment problems.

You should not rely exclusively on a single endpoint, interface, or website for mission-critical activity.

11. Third-Party Risk

LUST Chain users may interact with wallets, exchanges, bridges, analytics tools, dApps, liquidity pools, hosting providers, or community tools that are not controlled by the core project.

These services may fail, be hacked, misbehave, disappear, change terms, or expose users to additional legal or technical risk. The project is not responsible for third-party products or infrastructure.

12. Legal & Regulatory Risk

Digital asset regulation may change rapidly across jurisdictions. Depending on where you live, use of blockchain infrastructure, tokens, staking systems, DeFi tools, or ecosystem participation may be restricted, regulated, taxed, or prohibited.

You are solely responsible for understanding and complying with the laws applicable to you.

13. No Advice, No Guarantee, No Promise

Nothing in the project, whitepaper, website, documentation, explorer, RPC services, ecosystem applications, community channels, social media, or public communications should be interpreted as:

  • financial advice
  • investment advice
  • legal or tax advice
  • a guarantee of profit
  • a guarantee of listing
  • a guarantee of liquidity
  • a guarantee of technical stability
  • a guarantee of future development or adoption

All decisions involving LUST Chain are made at your own risk.

  • Website: https://lustchain.org

  • Platform: https://platform.lustchain.org
